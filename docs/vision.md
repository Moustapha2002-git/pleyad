# Vision

## The problem

Learning is fragmented. A motivated learner watches a YouTube series, buys a Udemy
course, audits an edX program, and follows a LinkedIn Learning path — and there is no
single place that understands the *whole* of what they are learning. Progress is
scattered. Nothing connects it. Organizations that sponsor learning (companies,
universities, ministries, academies) have even less visibility.

## The product

Pleyad is the connective layer. It sits above every learning platform and unifies the
learner's activity into one space:

- **Capture** — a browser extension detects learning activity on supported platforms and
  syncs it automatically. No manual entry.
- **Organize** — learners group activity into a personal library and playlists built
  around goals, not around the platform the content happens to live on.
- **Track** — one dashboard shows real progress across everything, everywhere.
- **Grow (later phases)** — mentors, AI companion, analytics, and organization-level
  programs build on top of this unified activity graph.

## Why this wins

The moat is not content — it's the **unified activity graph** per learner and per
organization, and the **distribution surface** (the extension) that keeps it fed. Once a
learner's whole learning life flows through Pleyad, every higher-order feature
(recommendations, mentoring, analytics, credentials) has uniquely rich data to work with.

## Who it's for

- **Learners** — one home for everything they study.
- **Organizations** — branded workspaces to run, sponsor, and measure learning:
  companies (L&D), universities, academies, ministries. Innovation Academy is the first.

## What Pleyad is NOT

- Not a course host / not another LMS competing on content.
- Not a single-brand app. Innovation Academy is a *tenant*, not the product.

## The one-line test for every decision

> "Does this strengthen the unified learning graph or the surface that feeds it, for
> **any** organization on the platform — or does it bake in a single-tenant assumption we'll
> have to tear out later?"
